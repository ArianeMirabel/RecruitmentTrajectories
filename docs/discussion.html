30 Years of Recruitment in Tropical Forest After Selective Logging

4 Discussion

  • Despite a higher number of recruits, the pool of recruited species is dominated by a smaller set of species than compared to natural forest ().

  • This is confirmed with the comparison to null model: in natural forest the recruitement equals a random sampling whereas it is less diverse after disturbance (,).

  • The turnover is high until 15 years after disturbance but then reveals a return to the initial state . The decreasing turnover fits the observed increase in whole plot diversity after disturbance, which suggests that recruitment dynamics are driven by competition while abiotic parameter remain stable (Anderson 2007)

  • The functional diversity of recruited trees also increases after disturbance, probably because of higher recruitment rates. It decreases more rapidly for the higher disturbance intensity, this reflects that some species dominate the recruitment when the intensity is high .

  • 30 years after disturbance it seems that the recruitment dynamics have recovered (,). But the post disturbance changes remain visible at the scale of the community (,,).

  • The big question is then: would we observe the same dynamic after a second cycle or is the seed bank depleted after the recruitment of old-growth species. HP: A second rotation would be lethal for the stand composition recovery.
