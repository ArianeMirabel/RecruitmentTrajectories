30 Years of Recruitment in Tropical Forest After Selective Logging

4 Discussion

From the 30 years of forest dynamics survey in the Paracou station we highlighted the contrasting recruitment mechanisms between control and post-disturbance tropical forests. In disturbed stands a significant selection that was positively correlated to the disturbance intensity increased the dominance among recruited trees. The response of functional diversity to disturbance was unimodal with a peak betweeen 5 and 10 years after disturbance and, for the lower disturbance, the maintenance of a higher than control stands until 30 years after disturbance. We suggest that the first increase in functional diversity is due to recruitment of a variety of saplings from the before-disturbance stand on hold of suitable growth condition. This would be followed by the recruitment of trees from the seed bank which germinated at the moment of the disturbance and undergo significant environmental filters selecting acquisitive strategies. For low disturbance intensity this second recruitment dynamics from the soil seed bank displayed a low species-abundance turnover (3.3) and fitted a random recruitment model (3.2). This suggested some recruitment limitation (???; ???) in the T1 plots, due to the short dispersal distance of tropical trees, that is consistent with the genetic clumping observed for some pioneer species after disturbance (Leclerc et al. 2015; Scotti et al. 2015) driving a rapid close of the forest (???). For intense disturbance the turnover proved significantly higher which suggested the role of competitive exclusions restricting the pool of recruited species as suggested by the lower diversity than obtained from a random null model and previously observed (Molino et Sabatier 2001). > Confirms the IDH and Molino results + “between patch” model for high richness (???; ???)

Whenever the disturbance intensity the species-abondance turnover decreased up to the initial and control stands values which argues for the recovery of the before-disturbance environment, which may vary or not in a tight relation to the stand (Anderson 2007) and the convergence of communities composition (???).

The difference in trajectories among treatments suggest the existence of a disturbance intensity threshold that would be between the first and second disturbance intensity applied here and involve the gaps size, the richness of exploited trees or the intervention density.
